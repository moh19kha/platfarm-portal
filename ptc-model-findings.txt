- NO company_id field (company comes from employee)

KEY FINDINGS:
1. pf.petty.cash has 3 transaction types: top_up, expense_deduction, adjustment
2. pf.petty.cash has expense_sheet_id linking to hr.expense.sheet — expense deductions relate to expense reports
3. pf.petty.cash.request state: [submitted, approved, refused] — NO draft
4. pf.petty.cash state: [draft, confirmed]
5. pf.petty.cash has topup_request_id linking to pf.petty.cash.request
6. The model ALREADY supports expense management via transaction_type='expense_deduction'
7. Neither model has company_id — company is derived from employee

DESIGN IMPLICATIONS:
- Petty Cash Top-Ups: pf.petty.cash.request (submitted→approved→refused) creates pf.petty.cash (type=top_up)
- Expense Deductions: pf.petty.cash (type=expense_deduction) links to hr.expense.sheet via expense_sheet_id
- The relationship is: employee gets top-up → balance increases → expense deduction → balance decreases
- We can use pf.petty.cash directly for expense claims without hr.expense.sheet
